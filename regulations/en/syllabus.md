## Aims

- Introduce biophysical foundations of cellular structures, selected tissues/organs and the organism as a whole.
- Explain physical phenomena occurring in the human body.
- Discuss mechanisms and effects of physical factors acting on the human body.
- Present physical principles of modern diagnostic and therapeutic methods.

## Course content

### Lectures

1. Laws of thermodynamics (1st and 2nd law, open systems, coupled processes).
2. Molecular interactions and their role in biological structures (electrostatics, H-bonds, van der Waals, hydrophobic effects).
3. Biological membranes: structure and properties (components, Singer–Nicolson model).
4. Membrane transport (passive, active).
5. Bioelectrical phenomena in membranes (ion channels, resting/action potentials).
6. Physical basics of NMR imaging and spectroscopy.
7. Effects of temperature and humidity on the human body (heat exchange, thermoregulation).
8. Effects of non-ionising radiation on the human body.

### Practicals

1. Radioactive decay law; radioisotopes; activity measurement.
2. Ionising radiation effects on matter (absorption, attenuation, medical use).
3. Radiation detection methods and measurement statistics.
4. Biophysics of the human voice.
5. Physical basics of ECG.
6. Blood pressure measurement.
7. Concentration measurements (spectrometry, polarimetry, refractometry).
8. Emission/absorption spectra analysis.
9. Image formation in optical systems.
10. Image generation in the human eye.
11. Light polarisation; optical isomerism.
12. Software-assisted recording/analysis (ECG, pulse, pH, etc.).
13. Laser beam attenuation in solids.

### Seminars

1. Introduction to mathematical methods used in Biophysics.

# Assessment conditions

## General rules

1. The course consists of laboratory classes (25h), seminars (10h) and lectures (10h), ending with an exam.
2. Students' attendance is mandatory. Each absence must be justified, and any missed lab class must be made up.
3. Lab classes are held at the Department of Biophysics.
4. Objectives of the first class: introduction to regulations, safety procedures, and division into groups.

## Requirements for obtaining credit

1. Lab classes are divided into 3 thematic modules.
2. Students' achievements are assessed based on theoretical preparation and practical performance (experiments, analysis of results, report).
3. Each student can receive 2 bonus points for outstanding performance at particular classes.
4. At the end of each semester, students must pass a test (maximum 40 points).
5. The final grade for lab classes is the sum of lab credits, bonus points and test points (maximum 58 points).
6. Students who have completed all classes and obtained at least 24 points are admitted to the final exam.
7. One resit opportunity is available for students with fewer than 24 points.
8. Students who have obtained a total of at least 42 points are exempt from the exam and receive a 'very good' grade (5).

## Exam

1. The exam is delivered as a test (multiple-choice, essay-type or calculation problems).
2. Students may review their exam papers at a date agreed by the Head of the Department of Biophysics and the students' representative.

# Rules and Regulations

Rules and Regulations for the Biophysics course

## General Rules & Regulations

1. The course in Biophysics consists of laboratory classes (25 hours), seminars (10) and lectures (10 hours), ending and with an exam.
2. Students' attendance is mandatory. Each absence must be justified and any missed lab class must be redone at a time agreed with the course teacher.
3. Lab classes are held at the Biophysics Department.
4. Objectives of the first lab class:

   - Introducing students to the rules and regulations of the course
   - Safety procedures at the laboratory
   - Dividing students into groups and assignment of tasks
   - Course overview

   Recommended reading:

   - Paul Davidovits – "Physics in Biology and Medicine"
   - Roland Glaser – "Biophysics"

## Requirements for obtaining a credit in Biophysics

5. Laboratory classes are divided into 3 thematic modules.
6. Students' achievements in the practical part are assessed on theoretical background and practical performance, including: Experiments (individual or group), analysis and presentation of results; report according to a given formula (available on the department website).
7. Each student can receive 2 bonus points for outstanding performance at particular lab meetings.
8. At the end of each semester, after lab practice, students must pass a test covering lab and lecture topics (max 40 points: 30 for practice, 10 for lectures).
9. Final grade for lab classes is the sum of lab credits, bonus points, and test points. Maximum:

   `18(3x6)+40=58`

10. Students who completed all lab classes and received minimum 24 points are allowed to take the final exam.
11. Two retakes are available for students with fewer than 24 test points. If still below 24, no credit is awarded and the student cannot take the final exam.
12. Students with at least 42 points (bonus + first attempt test) are exempt from the exam and receive the top grade (5). This does not apply to the resit test result.

## Final exam

13. The final exam includes mixed formats (multiple-choice, free text, calculation problems).
14. Students can review their papers at a time agreed by the Head of the Department and the students' representative.
15. Exam grading scale

| Range | Grade |
| --- | --- |
| 0–59% | 2 |
| 60–68% | 3 |
| 69–76% | 3+ |
| 77–84% | 4 |
| 85–90% | 4+ |
| 91–100% | 5 |

16. In all other matters, decisions rest with the Head of the Department of Biophysics.

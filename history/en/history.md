# History of the Department of Medical Biophysics

<!-- Editorial images can be added under images/ and referenced as ![caption](images/example.png). -->

The origins of today's Department of Medical Biophysics date back to the very beginning of the Medical Academy in Białystok. Below is a chronological outline of its history:

### 1950s – Beginnings and first research

- 1950: With the establishment of the Medical Academy in Białystok, the Department of Medical Physics begins its activities, primarily teaching students of the newly formed Faculty of Medicine.
- 1954: The Chair of Physics is established.
- 1955: Prof. Emanuel Trembaczowski becomes head of the Chair of Physics and initiates the first scientific studies on natural radioactivity.
- 1957: Thanks to Prof. Trembaczowski's efforts, the Didactic Isotope Laboratory is established — the first of its kind at medical universities in Poland — enabling expanded teaching and isotope-based research. Many doctoral and habilitation theses originated there.

### 1960s–70s – Renaming and staff development

- At the turn of the 1960s and 1970s, due to organizational changes, the Chair of Physics was dissolved, and the Department of Medical Physics was renamed the Department of Biophysics.
- During this period, the department employed, among others, Assoc. Prof. Stanisław Zenon Mnich and Prof. Franciszek Rogowski, who later became head of the Department of Nuclear Medicine.

### 1990s – New building, new research directions and new heads

- 1992: The department receives a new building with a modern gamma spectrometry laboratory, enabling research into environmental radioactive contamination, especially after the Chernobyl disaster.
- 1993: The Department of Biophysics is incorporated into an international early warning system for nuclear accidents.
- 1993: After the death of Prof. Emanuel Trembaczowski, Dr. Marek Zalewski temporarily serves as head of the Department of Biophysics.
- 1994: Dr. Anna Kostrzewska becomes head of the department. Under her leadership, research expands to biophysical aspects of smooth muscle physiology, and in 1995 a lab for recording and analyzing contractile activity is established.

### 21st century – Further development and present shape

- After Prof. Kostrzewska, the department was headed by Assoc. Prof. Maria Karpińska.
- Today, the unit operates as the Department of Medical Biophysics, headed by Assoc. Prof. Beata Modzelewska.

Current main research directions:

- Research on brain–computer interfaces (BCI).
- Pharmacodynamics of spasmolytic agents.
- Studies of smooth muscle contractile activity in vitro.
